## 4. Voice & Tone

### Brand Archetype: The Mentor (Sage + Caregiver)

**Primary Archetype: The Sage**
- **Core Motivation**: Seek truth and share knowledge
- **Goal**: Help others understand the world through wisdom and expertise
- **Strategy**: Continuous learning and thoughtful teaching
- **Message**: "The truth will set you free"

**Secondary Archetype: The Caregiver**
- **Core Motivation**: Protect and care for others
- **Goal**: Help students succeed and feel supported
- **Strategy**: Do things for others, provide guidance and resources
- **Message**: "I've got your back"

**Why This Combination Works for Job Club:**

The Mentor archetype blends the Sage's pursuit of knowledge with the Caregiver's nurturing support. This reflects the brand's dual nature:
1. **Sage qualities**: 23 years of expertise, research-backed insights, honest conversations about AI
2. **Caregiver qualities**: Student-focused support, welcoming community, personalized guidance

**How This Shows Up:**
- We share hard truths about AI and careers (Sage) but with encouragement and support (Caregiver)
- We provide expert knowledge (Sage) while making it accessible and approachable (Caregiver)
- We challenge students to think critically (Sage) while celebrating their progress (Caregiver)

**Brand Personality Traits:**
- **Wise**: Drawing from 23 years of experience and 10,000+ students
- **Honest**: "AI won't replace your job, but someone using AI might"
- **Supportive**: Creating safe spaces for learning and growth
- **Experienced**: Deep technical knowledge paired with teaching expertise
- **Grounded**: Realistic about challenges, optimistic about solutions

### Brand Voice Attributes

**ğŸ¯ Clear & Direct**
- No jargon or buzzwords
- Say what you mean
- Get to the point
- Example: "Learn AI skills" not "Synergize your AI competencies"

**ğŸ’¡ Realistic & Grounded**
- Acknowledge challenges
- Avoid hype
- Present balanced perspectives
- Example: "AI won't replace your job, but someone using AI might"

**ğŸ¤ Supportive & Encouraging**
- Welcoming to all levels
- Celebrate progress
- Normalize learning struggles
- Example: "Everyone starts somewhere. Let's learn together."

**ğŸ§  Thoughtful & Informed**
- Back claims with evidence
- Encourage critical thinking
- Provide context
- Example: "Here's what research shows..."

**ğŸŒŸ Approachable & Human**
- Write like a helpful peer
- Use contractions naturally
- Inject personality (without being unprofessional)
- Example: "We've got your back" vs. "We provide comprehensive support"

### Tone Variations by Context

**Homepage / Marketing**
- **Tone**: Inspiring, welcoming, confident
- **Goal**: Motivate action
- **Example**: "Ready to master AI and launch your career? Join 500+ students building their future."

**Onboarding**
- **Tone**: Friendly, reassuring, clear
- **Goal**: Reduce anxiety, encourage completion
- **Example**: "Great start! Let's find the right path for you."

**Educational Content**
- **Tone**: Informative, patient, thorough
- **Goal**: Facilitate learning
- **Example**: "Let's break this down step by step..."

**Error Messages**
- **Tone**: Helpful, constructive, calm
- **Goal**: Guide toward resolution
- **Example**: "Oops! That email format doesn't look right. Try: name@example.com"

**Success Messages**
- **Tone**: Celebratory, encouraging, motivating
- **Goal**: Reinforce positive behavior
- **Example**: "You did it! ğŸ‰ Your first project is live."

### Writing Guidelines

**Headlines & CTAs**
- Active voice: "Start Learning" not "Learning Can Be Started"
- Benefit-focused: "Land Your Dream Job" not "Job Preparation Available"
- Specific: "Join 500+ Students" not "Join Our Community"

**Body Copy**
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Scannable format (bullets, subheadings)
- Conversational but professional
- Second person ("you") when appropriate

**Microcopy**
- Helpful, not clever
- Guide user action clearly
- Anticipate questions
- Example: Button text: "Save Progress" vs. "Save"

### Words We Use

**âœ… Preferred**
- Career preparation (not job hunting)
- AI literacy (not AI mastery)
- Community (not network)
- Projects (not assignments)
- Portfolio (not resume)
- Learn (not study)
- Build (not complete)
- Students (not users)

**âŒ Avoid**
- Disrupt, revolutionize, transform (overused)
- Synergy, leverage, ecosystem (corporate jargon)
- Just, simply, easy (dismissive of difficulty)
- Obviously, clearly (assumes knowledge)
- Guru, ninja, rockstar (cringe-worthy)

## 6. Imagery & Illustrations

### Photography Guidelines

**Style**
- Authentic, not stock-photo-ish
- Diverse representation
- Professional but approachable
- Natural lighting preferred

**Usage**
- Blog headers
- Event promotions
- Member spotlights
- Marketing materials

### Illustrations

**Style**
- Modern, minimal
- Friendly and approachable
- Use brand colors
- Abstract or representational (not realistic)

**Usage**
- Empty states
- Onboarding screens
- Hero sections
- Feature explanations

### Image Treatment

**Aspect Ratios**
- Hero images: 16:9
- Card thumbnails: 4:3 or 1:1
- Profile photos: 1:1 (circular crop)

**Overlays** (when text on images)
- Dark overlay: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.4)
- Ensure text contrast: 4.5:1 minimum

## 7. Motion & Animation

### Principles
- **Purposeful**: Every animation should have a function
- **Quick**: Keep under 300ms for UI transitions
- **Subtle**: Don't distract from content
- **Consistent**: Use same easing across similar interactions

### Timing Functions

**Standard Easing**
```
ease-in-out: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)
```

**Quick Transitions** (hover, focus)
```
Duration: 150ms
Easing: ease-in-out
```

**Page Transitions**
```
Duration: 300ms
Easing: ease-in-out
```

### Common Animations

**Button Hover**
- Scale: 1.02
- Shadow: Increase
- Duration: 150ms

**Card Hover**
- Translate Y: -4px
- Shadow: Increase
- Duration: 200ms

**Modal Enter**
- Fade in: Opacity 0 â†’ 1

- Duration: 200ms

**Loading Spinner**
- Rotate: 360deg continuous
- Duration: 1s
- Easing: linear

## 8. Accessibility Standards

### WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

**Color Contrast**
- Text: 4.5:1 minimum
- Large text (18pt+): 3:1 minimum
- UI components: 3:1 minimum

**Keyboard Navigation**
- All interactive elements focusable
- Logical tab order
- Visible focus indicators
- Skip links available

**Screen Readers**
- Semantic HTML
- ARIA labels where needed
- Alt text for images
- Form labels properly associated

**Responsive Design**
- Text resizable to 200%
- No horizontal scroll
- Touch targets 44Ã—44px minimum
- Mobile-friendly navigation
